What is this?

GridSight forecasts Great Britain's national solar power output — half-hourly, 6–12 hours ahead — as a probabilistic range (p10 / p50 / p90), not a single guess.

What is NESO?

The National Energy System Operator (formerly National Grid ESO) runs GB's electricity system. It publishes its own embedded solar & wind forecast to balance the grid — the operational benchmark every model is judged against.

Beating NESO

Across 2 years of out-of-sample data, the stacked model (LightGBM + TCN, quantile) cuts NESO's mean absolute error roughly in half, and keeps ~9 in 10 actuals inside its p10–p90 band. Scrub the timeline — it holds across seasons.

Where the data comes from

Met Office UK-2km NWPAWS Open Data. Irradiance, cloud, temp, wind. New model run every 3 h — the forecast's weather driver.
PV_Live — Sheffield SolarActual GB solar generation by GSP, half-hourly (updates ~30 min). The ground truth & recent-history features.
NESO Data PortalEmbedded solar & wind forecast, hourly, out to 14 days. The baseline we compare against.

How it's built

Bronze → Silver → Gold parquet on HuggingFace. Features: NWP as-of-forecast (leak-safe), clear-sky, calendar, lagged output. Model: TCN-Q + LGBM-Q stacked by a linear quantile meta-learner. Served hourly on Azure Container Apps.
